Folic Acid (Vitamin B9)

Importance: Helps prevent neural tube defects in the developing baby, such as spina bifida.
Sources: Leafy green vegetables, citrus fruits, beans, fortified cereals, and supplements.

Iron

Importance: Supports the increased blood volume and helps deliver oxygen to the baby.
Sources: Red meat, poultry, fish, lentils, spinach, and iron-fortified cereals.

Calcium

Importance: Essential for the development of the baby’s bones and teeth and maintaining the mother’s bone health.
Sources: Dairy products (milk, cheese, yogurt), leafy greens, almonds, and fortified plant-based milk.

Vitamin D

Importance: Helps the body absorb calcium and supports bone health.
Sources: Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), fortified milk, eggs, and sunlight exposure.

Protein

Importance: Supports the growth of the baby’s tissues and organs and helps maintain maternal muscle mass.
Sources: Lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, beans, tofu, nuts, and dairy products.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA and EPA)

Importance: Crucial for brain and eye development in the baby.
Sources: Fatty fish (salmon, sardines), flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, and fish oil supplements.

Fiber

Importance: Helps prevent constipation, a common issue during pregnancy.
Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes, and seeds.

Vitamin C

Importance: Supports the immune system and helps the body absorb iron.
Sources: Citrus fruits (oranges, lemons), strawberries, bell peppers, broccoli, and tomatoes.

Vitamin A

Importance: Supports the development of the baby’s heart, lungs, kidneys, eyes, and bones.
Sources: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, and fortified dairy products. (Avoid excessive intake from animal sources like liver.)

Iodine

Importance: Essential for the baby’s brain development and regulation of the mother’s thyroid hormones.
Sources: Iodized salt, fish, dairy products, and seaweed.

Zinc

Importance: Supports the immune system and cell growth.
Sources: Meat, shellfish, legumes, seeds, and nuts.

Choline

Importance: Supports brain development and helps prevent neural tube defects.
Sources: Eggs, meat, fish, broccoli, and peanuts.

Magnesium

Importance: Helps regulate muscle and nerve function, blood sugar levels, and blood pressure.
Sources: Whole grains, nuts, seeds, legumes, and leafy green vegetables.

Potassium

Importance: Helps maintain proper fluid balance and supports healthy blood pressure.
Sources: Bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach, and oranges.
