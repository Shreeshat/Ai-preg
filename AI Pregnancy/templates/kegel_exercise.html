Pregnancy Exercises

Month 1 (Weeks 1-4)

Walking

A gentle walk for 20-30 minutes daily to maintain overall fitness and reduce stress.

Pelvic Tilts

Strengthens your back and abdominal muscles, helping relieve lower back pain.

Kegels

Enhances the strength of your pelvic floor muscles to support your growing uterus.

Note: Avoid high-impact exercises during the first month. Stay hydrated and rest as needed.

Month 2 (Weeks 5-8)

Swimming

A low-impact way to stay active while reducing joint stress and improving circulation.

Side-Lying Leg Lifts

Strengthens hips and thighs while providing balance and stability for your body.

Seated Upper-Body Exercises

Use light weights to improve upper body endurance and posture.

Tip: Wear supportive shoes and avoid lying flat on your back for long periods.

Month 3 (Weeks 9-12)

Cat-Cow Stretch

Relieve back tension and improve flexibility with this gentle yoga stretch.

Squats

Strengthen your pelvic muscles and prepare for labor with controlled squats.

Child's Pose

A relaxing stretch to ease back and hip discomfort.

Tip: Focus on relaxation exercises and stretches during the third month.

Month 4 (Weeks 13-16)

Bridges

Strengthen glutes and lower back while improving posture and stability.

Side Plank

Strengthens the core and improves side-to-side balance.

Modified Push-Ups

Target upper body strength while being gentle on the wrists.

Tip: Focus on gentle strengthening exercises as your body adjusts.

Month 5 (Weeks 17-20)

Wall Push-Ups

Strengthen arms and chest with wall push-ups that are gentle on your back.

Lunges

Improve leg strength and balance while preparing for labor.

Clamshells

Target the hip muscles for better pelvic support during pregnancy.

Tip: Keep your movements slow and controlled to avoid overstretching.

Month 6 (Weeks 21-24)

Squats

Continue strengthening lower body muscles while maintaining balance and stability.

Shoulder Press

Strengthen the upper body, focusing on shoulders and arms.

Leg Raises

Strengthens your lower abdominal muscles and helps with pelvic stability.

Tip: Practice deep breathing during exercises to reduce stress and anxiety.

Month 7 (Weeks 25-28)

Modified Seated Row

Engage your back and arms with gentle seated row variations.

Lunges

Enhance leg and pelvic strength for better support.

Side Plank

Strengthen your obliques for stability during the later stages of pregnancy.

Tip: Rest as needed to prevent overexertion.

Month 8 (Weeks 29-32)

Wall Push-Ups

Continue strengthening arms and chest while maintaining a stable posture.

Seated Leg Extensions

Strengthen thighs and maintain mobility as your belly grows.

Seated Marching

Improve circulation while keeping movements low-impact.

Tip: Focus on comfort and avoid strenuous activities that cause discomfort.

Month 9 (Weeks 33-36)

Walking

Maintain light activity with frequent walks, and focus on relaxation.

Child's Pose

A soothing stretch to ease back tension as your body prepares for labor.

Modified Squats

Strengthen the pelvic muscles and prepare for delivery.

Tip: Focus on gentle movements to keep energy levels balanced as the due date approaches.

Stay active, stay healthy! Always consult your healthcare provider before starting any exercise program during pregnancy.
